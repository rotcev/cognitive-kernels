## 1. 401(k) Contribution Limits & Employer Match Tax Treatment

### Contribution Limits
| Year | Employee Limit | Employer+Employee Combined | Age 50+ Catch-up |
|------|---|---|---|
| **2025** | $23,500 | $70,000 | +$7,500 |
| **2026** | $24,500 | $72,000 | +$8,000 |

**2026 Special Rule (SECURE 2.0):** Catch-up contributions for high earners ($150k+ prior-year wages) must be Roth basis (no pre-tax option).

### Employer Match Tax Treatment
- **For Employer:** Employer match is tax-deductible (business expense)
- **For Employee:** Pre-tax 401(k) match/contributions reduce taxable income immediately (deferred tax treatment)
- **Withdrawal Tax:** All distributions (contributions + growth) taxed as ordinary income when withdrawn in retirement
- **Relevant for $50k decision:** 4% employer match = $2,000-$2,400 free money (varies by salary), fully tax-sheltered now, ordinary income tax on withdrawal later

**Action for age 30 earner:** Prioritize capturing employer match first before side business savings.

## 2. Student Loan Interest Deduction

### Deduction Limits & Phase-Outs

| Aspect | 2025 | 2026 |
|--------|------|------|
| **Maximum Deduction** | $2,500 or actual interest paid (whichever less) | $2,500 or actual interest paid |
| **Single Filer Phase-Out Begins** | $85,000 MAGI | $85,000 MAGI |
| **Single Filer Phase-Out Complete** | $100,000 MAGI | $100,000 MAGI |
| **Married Filing Jointly Phase-Out Begins** | $170,000 MAGI | $175,000 MAGI |
| **Married Filing Jointly Phase-Out Complete** | $200,000 MAGI | $205,000 MAGI |

### Current Situation at Age 30
- **Income level:** Unknown from brief, but if $60k-$85k W-2 salary: **No phase-out, full deduction available**
- **$12k student loans @ 5.8%:** Est. ~$700/year interest if in early repayment
- **Side business income:** May push into phase-out zone if combined MAGI exceeds thresholds
- **Critical point:** Phase-out is based on MAGI (Modified Adjusted Gross Income), which includes side business net income

### Calculation Example
If single, $65k W-2 + $20k net side business = $85k MAGI → **deduction begins phasing out**
If side business grows to $25k net → $90k MAGI → **partial deduction loss (50% of excess over $85k)**

**Action:** Monitor MAGI carefully; side business growth may eliminate this deduction.

## 3. Self-Employment (SE) Tax

### SE Tax Rates & Thresholds

**Tax Components:**
- **Social Security portion:** 12.4% (on wages up to Social Security wage base)
- **Medicare portion:** 2.9% (no income limit) + 0.9% Additional Medicare Tax (high earners)
- **Total standard rate:** 15.3% on net self-employment income
- **Minimum filing requirement:** $400+ in net SE income

### Social Security Wage Base (determines SS tax cap)
| Year | Wage Base | Implication |
|------|-----------|------------|
| **2025** | $176,100 | SS tax only applies to first $176.1k of SE income; Medicare applies to all |
| **2026** | $184,500 | SS tax capped at first $184.5k of SE income |

### Key SE Tax Facts
1. **Deductible portion:** You can deduct 50% of SE tax paid as an income tax deduction (adjustment to income on Form 1040)
2. **Applied to:** 92.35% of net self-employment income (not 100%)
3. **Additional Medicare Tax:** 0.9% kicks in for single filers at $200k+ income (W-2 + SE combined)

### Numerical Example: $30k Side Business Net Income
- Net self-employment income: $30,000
- Applies to: $30,000 × 92.35% = $27,705 (SE tax base)
- SE Tax = $27,705 × 15.3% = $4,240
- Tax deductible portion = $4,240 ÷ 2 = $2,120 (reduces taxable income)
- **Net cost:** ~$2,120 in actual tax burden to you

**Action for $50k allocation:** SE tax is a significant cost. Every $10k of side business = ~$1,400 in SE taxes.

## 4. Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction (Section 199A)

### 20% QBI Deduction Overview
- Deduct up to **20% of Qualified Business Income** (+ 20% of REIT dividends, PTP income)
- Permanent (made permanent by One Big Beautiful Bill Act in 2025)
- Applies to **pass-through entities:** sole proprietorships, S-corps, partnerships, LLCs
- **Does NOT apply to** W-2 wage income

### 2025 Income Thresholds

| Filing Status | Full Deduction Available | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Completes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single** | ≤$197,300 | $197,300 | $247,300 |
| **Married Filing Jointly** | ≤$394,600 | $394,600 | $494,600 |

**At or below thresholds:** Take 20% deduction with minimal additional calculations
**Above thresholds:** Subject to W-2 wage and depreciable property limitations (complex)

### 2026 Income Thresholds (Adjusted for Inflation)

| Filing Status | Full Deduction Available | Phase-Out Begins | Phase-Out Completes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Single** | ≤$200,000 (approx) | $200,000 | $275,000 |
| **Married Filing Jointly** | ≤$400,000 (approx) | $400,000 | $544,600 |

**2026 New Minimum Deduction:** If you have $1k+ QBI and materially participate, minimum deduction = $400/year (inflation-adjusted annually)

### Simplified Example: $30k Side Business, Single Filer, $65k W-2
- W-2 income: $65,000
- Side business net income: $30,000
- **Total taxable income before QBI:** $95,000
- **QBI:** 20% × $30,000 = **$6,000 deduction** (no limitations since below $197.3k threshold)
- **Tax savings (25% bracket):** ~$1,500

**Action for $50k decision:** Side business QBI deduction can offset SE taxes. $30k business = ~$6k deduction = ~$1.5k tax savings.

## 5. Home Office Deduction

### Two Calculation Methods

#### A. Simplified Method
| Year | Rate Per Sq Ft | Max Deduction | Max Area |
|------|---|---|---|
| **2025** | $5/sq ft | $1,500 | 300 sq ft |
| **2026** | $6/sq ft | $1,800 | 300 sq ft |

**Calculation:** Dedicated home office square footage × $6/sq ft (2026)
**Example:** 200 sq ft office × $6 = $1,200 deduction (simple, no receipts)

#### B. Actual Expense Method
Deduct actual costs proportionally:
- Rent/mortgage interest (your share)
- Utilities (internet, electric, heating)
- Property taxes (your share)
- Insurance
- Maintenance/repairs
- Depreciation (if home is business property)
- Home office % = office sq ft ÷ total home sq ft

**Example:** 200 sq ft in 2,000 sq ft home = 10% allocation
- $1,000/month rent = $100 home office allocation
- $150/month utilities = $15 allocation
- Total: ~$115/month = $1,380/year

### Eligibility Rules
- **Must use space exclusively and regularly** for business
- Cannot use simplified method if prior year claimed depreciation
- **Employee remote workers:** Cannot claim (Tax Cuts & Jobs Act suspended this 2018-2025+)
- **Self-employed/business owners:** Full eligibility

### Strategic Choice
- **Simplified:** Better if home office is small (< 150 sq ft) or if actual expenses are low
- **Actual expenses:** Better if rent/mortgage + utilities high and office is large

**Action for $50k decision:** If setting up side business with dedicated home office, this adds another $1,200-$1,800/year deduction (depending on space & year).

## 6. SYNTHESIS: Impact on $50k Allocation Decision

### Tax Planning Priorities for Age 30, W-2 Job + Side Business Intent

1. **401(k) First:** Capture employer match immediately (~$2-2.4k free money) via W-2 job
   - Reduces both income tax and Self-Employment tax exposure
   - Use roughly $2,400-$3,000 of the $50k here

2. **Student Loan Interest Deduction:** Worth ~$500-700/year tax savings currently
   - At risk if side business income pushes MAGI above $100k (single) or $205k (married)
   - Monitor combined W-2 + side business MAGI

3. **Side Business SE Tax Cost:** Critical variable
   - Every $10k net business income = ~$1,400 SE tax liability
   - Partially offset by QBI 20% deduction (~$2k net income → $400 deduction = $100 tax savings)
   - If business failure: SE taxes already owed on income earned (not recoverable)

4. **QBI Deduction:** Significant if side business succeeds
   - $20k side business = $4k deduction = ~$1k tax savings
   - $30k side business = $6k deduction = ~$1.5k tax savings
   - Grows with business scale

5. **Home Office Deduction:** Quick tax win if physical office required
   - $1,200-$1,800 annual deduction (2025-2026)
   - Simple to claim with simplified method

### Allocation Strategy Framework

**Conservative ($50k on W-2 side):**
- $2,500 → 401(k) (capture match, tax shelter)
- $12,000 → Student loan principal (eliminate 5.8% debt)
- $35,500 → Emergency fund/low-risk savings
- Side business → Bootstrap with revenue, minimize upfront capital
- **Tax impact:** Student loan interest deduction intact, SE tax minimal if business slow-starts

**Growth ($50k on side business):**
- $2,500 → 401(k) (capture match)
- $12,000 → Student loan principal
- $35,500 → Dedicated business capital (equipment, marketing, home office setup)
- **Tax impact:** Higher SE taxes (cost $1.5-2k for each $10k net business income), but QBI deduction offsets 20-30%, home office deduction available immediately
- **Risk:** If business fails, SE taxes still due on any income generated; loan paydown stalled
